While in the Glassboro Public library maker
space, I met the owners of Escape Room South
Jersey who were having multiple electronic
challenges regarding circuitry. After a brief
conversation, I was asked to join the team.

This is the primary circuit I was brought on board to build.
A button pull-down circuit is used to regulate inputs
solely to on and off signals. This simple circuit
is the cornerstone of all the work I have done for the escape room.

Throughout my time at Escape Room South Jersey, I have built
in excess of 20 of these pull-down circuits to connect puzzle
sensors to the Escape Room Master Program. This task
has greatly refined my soldering skills.

For various puzzles, certain sensors are
used to advance through the escape room game.
These sensors transmit electrical data through the circuit
to the Arduino microcontroller to manipulate software.

Reading the input data from Arduino's I/O pins
the custom developed software wirelessly communicates
commands to the Escape Room Master program.
These commands trigger events in the game.

For example: the printing press combinational
lock uses the input of four buttons in a specific
order to trigger a solenoid lock. This opens
a hatch to reveal a key for the next puzzle.

Additional example: each room uses a Reed switch
to activate the commencement sequence for the game.
Pictured here, the Jersey Devil's Cabin room
start switch is tucked into the facade.
